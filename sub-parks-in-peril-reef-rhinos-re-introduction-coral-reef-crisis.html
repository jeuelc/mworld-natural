Coral Reef Crisis

The Great Barrier Reef is a marine park and World Heritage Area. It's the world's largest coral reef system about the size of Germany! This makes it the world's largest living structure. It has 600 types of corals and is home to thousands of species of jellyfish, sharks, whales, dolphins, sea turtles and, of course, the rainbow of fish!

People visit the reef to snorkel among the brightly coloured fish and visit the highly threatened coral. The ocean is becoming more acidic, and this, along with increasing temperatures, pollution and invasive species, threaten coral reefs the world over. Imagine if we lost all the coral in our oceans a huge diversity of fish, moray eels and jellyfish would have nowhere to live and the bigger fish like whales and dolphins would have nothing to feed on!

Marine parks like the Great Barrier Reef are especially sensitive to the effects of climate change. Rises in the ocean temperature can cause coral bleaching where the coral turns white and dies.
