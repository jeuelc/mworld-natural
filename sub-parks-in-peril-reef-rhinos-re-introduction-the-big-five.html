The Big Five

The Kruger National Park is in the north-east corner of South Africa, stretching along the Mozambique border to the east and Zimbabwe to the north. It is 19,485 km2, or about the size of Wales! It was established in 1926, making it South Africa's oldest national park.

Kruger is best known as a place to see 'the big five' the species that big game hunters used to hunt. Today they are the species that tourists most like to see: lion, African elephant, Cape buffalo, black rhinoceros and leopard.

Kruger is a hub of biodiversity but that's not all it is! It also contains some amazing and important rock art. The San people were hunter gatherers who lived in the area for 20,000 years before it became a park. They left a rich history of artwork which can be found here, if you look carefully!
