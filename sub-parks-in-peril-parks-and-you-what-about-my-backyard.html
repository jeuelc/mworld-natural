Urban green spaces are patches of habitat left in cities and residential areas. These can be important stepping stones for wildlife moving across the landscape, or habitats for some species. For people living in the city, they can be the only place to see plants and animals in a semi-natural setting. Interacting with nature is good for you! Your physical health is better, your brain works better and your psychological wellbeing is better.

Even though some of these green spaces are not formally protected parks, they can contribute a lot to biodiversity and your quality of life.

Even your garden can be an important wildlife refuge or miniature protected area. If you fill your garden with native plants and lots of nooks and crannies to create microhabitats, lizards, frogs, butterflies, beetles and birds will flock to pay a visit and perhaps even move in! Give them some rotting logs, leaf litter and a small garden pond and watch what happens! But remember, if you have cats they should be indoors at night, so that they don't hunt all of the critters living in your park!
