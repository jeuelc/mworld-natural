Who moves all that sand around in the desert? Who polished those pebbles in the river, or dumped that mud in the swamp? It all seems like a lot of effort! All of these were done by the relentless hand of nature: constantly moving, sorting and sculpting sediments.

The arrangements, sizes and shapes of sediments in a sedimentary rock are controlled by three main factors: 1) the speed of the water or air they were carried in; 2) the time the sediments spent being dragged around before being buried in their final resting place; and 3) how much rough treatment the sediments can withstand. It's not easy being sedimentary!

Because this sorting and shaping is driven by physical processes, and the laws of physics apply everywhere, you can find similar sediments on other planets!
