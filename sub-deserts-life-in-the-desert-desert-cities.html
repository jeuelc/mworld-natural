Desert Cities

Nowadays, in many deserts, towns and cities are being built for people who enjoy the sunshine, for tourists and holidaymakers, or for people who work in desert mines. Finding water for a desert town can be difficult! Sometimes deep bores pump water from underground, but if too much is taken, the supply can run out. Some cities rely on desalinated sea water, if the ocean is close by. Food may have to be delivered from farms hundreds or even thousands of kilometres away, in areas that receive more rain.

Some of the fastest-growing cities today are in desert areas. An example is the city of Riyadh in Saudi Arabia. Riyadh now has a population of more than 5 million and is still growing rapidly. The residents of Riyadh depend mainly on desalinated sea water, mixed with some water pumped from underground aquifers - rocks that contain microscopic pores or cracks which underground water seeps through.
