Rocks are made of smaller particles called minerals. The types and arrangement of minerals in a rock help geologists work out which primary rock type it belongs to. They could be:

1) Igneous rocks formed by cooling of molten rock called 'magma' (when underground) or from 'lava' (when above the ground);

2) Sedimentary rocks made from fragments of other rocks, which are then deposited on the Earth's surface;

3) Metamorphic rocks these have been put under high pressure and temperature, and the minerals have started to 'morph' from their original shape or composition.

Minerals come in many different types some are hard like diamond, others are soft enough to scratch with your fingernail like the 'graphite' in a pencil. Some minerals are clear like 'quartz', some are metallic and dark like the iron mineral 'hematite'.
