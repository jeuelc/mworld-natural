Water: Little or None!

 
click here

Can you imagine living in a hot desert but never quenching your thirst with a cool drink? Remarkably, some animals can do this.

Kangaroo rats, small rodents that live in burrows in the Chihuahuan Desert, obtain all the moisture they need from their food. They mostly eat seeds, but supplement this with some green vegetation and the occasional insect. They conserve water by only emerging from their burrows after sunset, so that they avoid the hottest part of the day. Kangaroo rats also have very few sweat glands, and their oily skin also helps them avoid drying out. They also have the most concentrated urine in the world!

Some small desert finches and other seed-eating birds can also live without ever drinking, but how they manage this is not yet fully understood. Perhaps you could solve this puzzle one day!
