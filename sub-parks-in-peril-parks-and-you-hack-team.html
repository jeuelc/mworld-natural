One very valuable job that volunteer groups do is to keep an eye out for invasive plants and animals and report sightings to the local park managers. Sometimes they even work with park rangers as part of 'hack teams' to pull out weeds and replace them with native plants.

The situation is so bad in some parks that there are almost as many invasive plant species as there are natives! Some invasive species tend to grow and spread aggressively and out-compete native species.

Stopping exotic species from coming in to the park in the first place is the best solution. You can help do this by making sure you clean your hiking boots or mountain bike tyres between trips to different parks. Seeds can get trapped in the mud on your boots or tyres and travel along with you as you move about. They fall off and start to grow and could become a new problem plant in your favourite park.
