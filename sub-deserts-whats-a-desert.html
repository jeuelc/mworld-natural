What's a Desert?

About a third of all the land on Earth is desert. Even though they can be uncomfortably hot and dry, deserts are home to many people, plants and animals. Shelter, food, and water are the most precious possessions for people who live in deserts.

Deserts can be hot! The record temperature was set in Death Valley in California, where in 1913 it reached 56.7°C in the shade! At night, deserts can become very cold, with temperatures below freezing point. The extreme range in a single day makes desert climates especially harsh.

The other thing that distinguishes deserts is the lack of vegetation. There may be few if any large trees, and a lot of bare ground or rock.

Although not ideal for farming, some deserts are very rich in minerals and other useful resources. One resource available in deserts is sunshine. This makes deserts handy places for generating solar power, which is important to people everywhere.
