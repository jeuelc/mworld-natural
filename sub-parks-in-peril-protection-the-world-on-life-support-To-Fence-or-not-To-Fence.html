To Fence or Not To Fence?

 
click here

With a population of seven billion people that's growing fast, the human race needs a LOT of resources. Some people think that parks, with their strong ecosystems and abundance of resources, should be available for grazing cattle, mining, harvesting timber or collecting animals and plants. These are called 'provisioning services' and lots of local communities use their parks for provisioning.

It is a pretty tough balancing act to protect the biodiversity of parks while also making sure that people aren't driven to illegal poaching or harvesting to get the resources they need.

Sometimes park rangers, ecologists and governments need to get creative!

Some parks control activities using permits. Others provide alternatives, such as creating plant nurseries outside parks for traditional medical plants. And some parks involve local communities in decision-making and in managing the park. This can provide opportunities for people to work in tourism and to benefit from visitors attracted to the park. Some governments even pay local people money to stop taking resources and animals from parks.
