Making an Earth Cake

Just like a cake, when you make a stack of layered rocks, you start with the base first, and work your way up.

In this way, you can usually tell which rocks are the oldest, and which are the youngest, merely by seeing which layers are at the bottom and which are at the top. Sounds easy, right?

However, sometimes the rocks can look like some deliciously disastrous baking accident, and working out which way was up can be a bit tricky!

But there are lots of clues written into the rocks which can tell you which way was up.
