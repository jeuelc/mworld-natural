Safeguarding our Deserts

 
click here

To many of us, deserts are remote and a little bit mysterious. Would you like to go and see a desert, to see for yourself how people and animals live there?

If you do travel into a desert, be careful not to drive over the desert plants, or step on them. They often grow slowly, battling heat and dryness. If you squashed one, it could take many years for it to recover! Deserts can be quite fragile, so it is wise to stay on the roads or walking paths. Too many tourists, with buses, cars, and walking boots, can be a problem for deserts. Cattle, sheep, and goats are often grazed in the deserts, but these too can cause damage to plants and soils. We need to take care of these special, dry places, so that people in the future can see them too. Understanding how deserts work is the best aid to managing them for the future.
