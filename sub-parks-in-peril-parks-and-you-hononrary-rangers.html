Some parks have volunteer programmes where you could become a citizen ranger or honorary ranger you may even get to wear a park uniform if you help out on weekends.

You could help with fundraising for the park, show park visitors around, or maybe take part in a 'bioblitz'!

A bioblitz is an intense period of biological surveying when people try to record all the living species within an area! Groups of scientists and volunteers set a short space of time (usually 24 hours) and record as much as they can see. Many countries run bioblitzes, including Australia, New Zealand, Taiwan, Trinidad and Tobago.

You could also become a 'citizen scientist'. This is where members of the public, like you, get to take part in scientific research. You could help to collect information used to better understand and protect the park. For example, you could help count numbers of animals or plants, such as little penguins, fruit bats or rare orchids.
