Imagine you're a ranger at Table Mountain National Park in South Africa. You've noticed that populations of the endangered African Penguin have been declining over the past few years. Why do you think this might be?

We know that Cape Town has been getting hotter maybe the heat has been damaging the eggs or killing the chicks. Hmmmm... how can you let people know about the plight of the African Penguin? A good way might be to show the penguins to visitors maybe they will be inspired to help in the conservation effort!

Park rangers also do a lot of work that doesn't involve animals at all, like patrolling the walkways to make sure visitors aren't trampling the beach and frightening animals. You might also need to travel deep into the strictly protected areas of the park to clear invasive weeds. Or you may have to fix a broken water main to make sure the toilets work for the visitors! There's a lot more to being a park ranger than meets the eye!
