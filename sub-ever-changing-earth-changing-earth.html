Changing Earth

Rocks and fossils have recorded important episodes of evolutionary and environmental change throughout Earth's history. Times of both blisteringly hot and bitterly cold global temperatures, of continental collision and breakup, and of biological 'boom and bust'.

Over the longest time periods, the slow and steady shifting of the continents can gradually change frozen tundra into a tropical paradise for plants and animals to thrive in. On the shortest timespans however, dramatic environmental jolts will devastate the organisms if they are too rapid, causing the deaths of entire species. These extinctions are 'geologically' sudden, often taking hundreds or even thousands of years, but compared to the age of the Earth, this is the blink of an eye!
