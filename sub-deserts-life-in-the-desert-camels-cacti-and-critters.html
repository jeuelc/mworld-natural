Camels, Cacti and Critters

Living things in deserts are special. Some plants, like the giant Saguaro cacti of the Sonoran Desert, can store thousands of litres of water inside their tissue. Their shallow, spreading root systems gather this water quickly when it rains. This is a kind of insurance, in case it does not rain again for some time. Other desert plants have special cells that can survive the heat and intense sunshine.

Desert animals, called 'xerocoles', have special adaptations to hot and dry conditions. Their body uses water efficiently, and they excrete unusually dry wastes to further conserve moisture. Some animals hardly ever drink water at all! Instead, they get sufficient moisture from the food they eat. But when it does not rain for a long time, it can get so dry that even the hardy desert plants and animals begin to die. If they can, animals may move to other areas to escape the dry conditions.
