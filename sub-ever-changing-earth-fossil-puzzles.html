Fossils are signs of ancient life. They are like puzzle pieces which, when gathered together, give us a picture of prehistoric life and the way life has evolved throughout Earth's history. They can tell you what extinct animals, plants and other organisms looked like, how they lived and how they related to others.

Just as fossils tell us about extinct (or living) critters, they can also tell us about 'extinct' environments: conditions on the Earth that are completely different to any that we know today.
