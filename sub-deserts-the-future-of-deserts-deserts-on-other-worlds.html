Deserts on Other Worlds

 
click here click here

Empty deserts have other uses that you might not think of. Astrobiologists examine Earth's deserts, because the harsh conditions help them understand how life might exist and be found on the waterless surface of the Moon, Mars or beyond. Deserts are also good places to test landing modules and rovers before they are risked on interplanetary missions.

What's more, because they have so few clouds, deserts are great places for astronomers. Telescopes can see into the night sky clearly from deserts. If you go to a desert, go outside at night and look up. You will have a great view of the Milky Way and almost certainly see satellites moving slowly across the sky. If you are lucky, you might also see a bright shooting star, or the International Space Station as it races by.
