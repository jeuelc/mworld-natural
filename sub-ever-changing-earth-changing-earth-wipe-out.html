Plants and animals have evolved to suit their environment ... but disaster occurs when the environment changes too quickly for organisms to adjust: a sure way to cause an extinction at the local scale or the entire species!

Remember that sediments are laid down over time: the longer the time frame, the more sediment is deposited ... so sudden environmental changes, like the ones that cause extinctions, are shown by thin rock layers. It's like a complex tragedy written in very fine print!

At the boundary line in the rock between the Cretaceous and the Palaeogene Periods (or 'K-Pg boundary') 66 million years ago, one such sudden event took place. The first clue that something disastrous happened was that dinosaur and other large reptilian fossils were abundant below the boundary, and completely missing from the rocks above it! But the real devastation is hidden in the details... tap the cards to take a closer look!
