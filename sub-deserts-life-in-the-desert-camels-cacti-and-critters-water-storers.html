Water Storers

 
click here click here

Rain in deserts is unreliable: sometimes it rains for several days in a row, and at other times, there is no rain for months. Some plants take out a kind of insurance policy to make sure that they don't run out of water.

The giant Saguaro cactus grows in the Sonoran Desert in Arizona and Mexico. When it rains, this cactus soaks up water from the wet soil and stores it in masses of pulpy flesh. The Saguaro has a kind of concertina skin that allows the whole plant to swell up. When rain does not fall, birds and animals eat the moist flesh of the cactus, so that the stored water helps them survive, as well.
