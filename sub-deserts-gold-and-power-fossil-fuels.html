Fossil Fuels

The oil found under many desert countries is used around the world to fuel buses, cars, and trucks. Fuel is so vital for modern life that countries sometimes fight over access to oil and gas. Many countries have little or no oil themselves, and rely on importing it from the oil-rich deserts. Why are the desert countries like Saudi Arabia and Iran so rich in oil and gas?

The answer takes us back many millions of years to a time when these areas were covered by shallow seas. These oceans were at that time located in the tropics. In these seas, the remains of abundant plants and animals settled to the bottom, and were buried. Through time, their remains became oil and gas. Since then, the old sea floors have become land desert land and the continents have shifted to their present locations. The valuable oil is still there, deep underground.
