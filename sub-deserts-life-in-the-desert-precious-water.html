Precious Water

Though deserts don't receive much rain, they have other sources of precious water. Some, like the Gobi Desert in Mongolia, have streams that are fed by melting snow in nearby high mountains. Elsewhere, water can seep through rocks, deep underground. This can trickle up to the surface to form a natural spring or 'oasis', or be reached by constructing deep wells.

Sometimes rivers flow into deserts from areas far away where the rainfall is larger. As they flow across the desert, they slowly dry out in the hot sun, and also shrink because their water soaks into the ground. Plants often line these rivers, and their roots seek out water stored in the river bed.

Some desert plants use dew, and others trap small droplets of water from fog that blows across the desert. Desert people are also learning to make use of dew and fog. Carefully collected dew can provide extra water for drinking or growing vegetables.
