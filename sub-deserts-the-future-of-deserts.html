The Future of Deserts

People who live in wet areas that have rivers, lakes, and fertile farms, often think of deserts as empty places that aren't much use for anything. But the special desert plants and animals need dry conditions. They are specially equipped for surviving with limited water. They would not know how to live in a place where it rained all the time!

People in the future will also depend on deserts. The sunny days will allow solar power to be made in abundance, and this will be used in towns and cities far away. Power from the deserts, including geothermal power, will be especially important as the underground oil and gas reserves begin to run out. In the future, we may also grow crops in the deserts, by careful irrigation. As the number of people in the world increases, the cultivation of deserts may help to feed people who have never seen or visited a desert.
