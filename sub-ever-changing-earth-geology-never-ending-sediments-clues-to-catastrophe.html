Clues to Catastrophe

 
click here

Rock layers record the environments of Earth's past, even during times of dramatic change! In fact, the biggest and baddest events leave the most spectacular rock formations. Most rock formation is a slow, steady process... but when a volcano erupts, an earthquake triggers a landslide, or an avalanche tumbles down a mountain, these rapid events can move an enormous amount of sediment in the blink of an eye. At the same time, these violent events cause erosion, whereby rocks are removed from their original position, disrupting many long years of previous rock formation. This creates a time-gap, or 'erosional unconformity' in the rock layers.

Once things have settled down, the normal slow rock formation takes over again. The 'disaster sediments' get buried and form sedimentary rocks, leaving clues of the catastrophe behind.
