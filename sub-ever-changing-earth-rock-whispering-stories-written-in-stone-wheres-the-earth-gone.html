Where's the sea gone?

The Himalayan mountain chain of Asia includes the highest mountain peaks on Earth, like Mt Everest, which reaches nearly nine kilometres above sea level!

Today, fast-flowing rivers, such as the Ganges River, pour out of the Himalaya carrying lots of sediment into the surrounding valleys. These rivers provide nutrients for the farms of India, China, Bangladesh, Pakistan, Nepal and Bhutan which, in turn, feed hundreds of millions of people.

But the land in this region hasn't always towered so high... in fact, this region used to be open sea, and many of the rocks that make up the Himalayas have come from the depths of an ocean that is now long extinct!

How do we know about this ancient ocean if it is long gone? Where did this ocean go? How did these rocks end up forming such high mountains? Tap the cards for clues...
