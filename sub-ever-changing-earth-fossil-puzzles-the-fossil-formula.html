The Fossil Formula

Fossils are the product of very special conditions. To become a fossil, a dead organism needs to avoid being broken apart in a harsh environment, hide from scavengers, and bacterial decay. Even after the organism is buried, the trouble isn't over! If the fossil is buried too deeply, it may become cooked and ruined.

The type of organism is an important factor too - those with hard parts, such as shells, wood, or bone, are far more likely to end up preserved as fossils. This is because they can resist the physical and chemical stress of being buried for millions of years. Soft and squishy organisms are less likely to be preserved!

For these reasons, we have found fossils for less than 1% of all species that have ever lived on Earth! The adventure of a fossil is a long and hard one, so the survivors are rare and precious!
