Nomadic Life

Traditionally, some desert people had no permanent home, but moved to wherever there had been rain, and grass for their animals. But this nomadic way of life is becoming less appealing as roads and towns spread into the desert. Imagine arriving at one of the best places for your sheep or goats to graze, only to find that it had been covered by buildings or car parks. Then where would you go?

In some deserts, people built permanent villages and towns. These were often located at a place that gave the people access to a source of water. A suitable location would be in a valley or 'wadi' (an Arabic word) that might receive runoff from surrounding hills. People store flood water in large underground chambers cut into the rock, to prevent evaporation and make the water last longer. Some of this water might be used for washing and cooking, to grow crop plants such as dates, and for watering stock.
