Parks come in all shapes and sizes. They can be big or small and contain mountains, forests, plains or even parts of the oceans.

Marine national parks can vary almost as much as those on land. They can include coastlines, areas near the shore or offshore areas that are important to life such as coral reefs or fish spawning areas.

Parks on land can include water catchments, river systems, wetlands, mountain ranges, forests, deserts, grasslands, volcanoes, caves, springs, waterfalls and woodlands. Even parts of Antarctica and the Arctic are protected.

The type and location of a park can influence the form or level of protection that it is given. There are no-take sanctuaries, sacred natural sites, peace parks or multiple use landscapes or seascapes. These areas can be managed by almost anyone governments, local communities, indigenous peoples, non-government organisations or members of the public, like you!
