Layer upon layer

If you look at a canyon, cliff face or road-cutting through a hill, the layers of the Earth are usually easy to see, like a cake that has been sliced open by a giant knife! Up close, you will see that most rock layers are actually made up of lots of smaller particles, called 'sediments'. The sediments can be tiny like those in mud, but others can be large like pebbles or even boulders!

Have you ever sat on sand at the beach, picked up pebbles from a streambed, or squished mud between your toes when wading into a lake? These are all sediments, and when stacked on top of each other, the sediments from these environments form into 'sedimentary rocks' over millions of years.

These layers of sedimentary rocks tell us the history of the Earth's environments, and the way the environment has changed through time!
