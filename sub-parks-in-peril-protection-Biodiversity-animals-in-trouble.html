The Kruger National Park in South Africa has a long history of protecting land and conserving threatened species. It has the largest rhino population in the world and, unlike many other parks, its elephant population is thriving.

But the animals of Kruger are threatened by illegal poaching. Some people believe that rhino horns can be a kind of medicine (though science has found no evidence for this) and this demand drives rhino poaching. Rhino horn is sold for thousands of dollars per kilogram on the black (illegal) market this means that it's worth more than gold.

Although the trade in rhino horn is illegal, poachers sometimes enter parks at night to shoot rhinos and cut off their horns. The rhinos rarely survive.

Black rhinos are critically endangered animals. There are fewer than 5,000 left in the wild.

Kruger is not the only park where poaching or illegal harvesting is a problem, and trying to stop it is a big challenge for the conservation effort.
