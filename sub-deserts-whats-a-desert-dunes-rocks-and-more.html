Deserts don't all look the same. Some consist mostly of rugged, rocky hills or mountains. These can be the source of streams and rivers, because being high, they receive a little bit more rain than the plains. This is called 'orographic precipitation'. Other deserts are flat and covered in small stones, and some just have sand dunes stretching to the horizon. Big sand deserts are called 'ergs' or 'sand seas'. The largest of these is the Rub' al Khali or 'the empty quarter'. It covers much of the Arabian Peninsula.

There are occasional oases in deserts. However, cooling off with a swim in a desert lake or river is usually just a dream, because most of the time they are completely dry. Just sometimes, in shady places, pools of water are left by the rain. These are important because they can provide birds and animals with drinking water. While they last, some birds gather mud from around such pools to build their nests.
