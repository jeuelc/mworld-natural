Categories

Are the Galapagos Islands protected in the same way as your local park? What about the Great Barrier Reef? Different types of parks are protected in different ways depending on their size, fragility and importance.

Across the world people think of parks differently, but the International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN) has developed categories for different types of parks which most people now use. This lets us know what type of park we are in, no matter where we are in the world.

There are seven categories of protected area. They go from 'Strict nature reserves where all access is regulated' (thats level Ia) down to 'Protected areas with sustainable use of natural resources' (thats level VI). This lowest level means that people can use the resources found in the park. The category that a park falls into depends on why it was made a park in the first place and the way that it is managed.

Parks can be managed by different groups, and sometimes more than one group will work together to manage a park. For example, a government may work alongside a local community to manage a successful park that contains important resources.
