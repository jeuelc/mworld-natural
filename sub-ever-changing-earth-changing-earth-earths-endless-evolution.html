Today, all the continents are moving, but at an incredibly slow pace: less than 10 centimetres per year! As slow as this is, the Earth is ever so patient and, as you read this, the continents are heading for a collision... but there's no need for a seatbelt!

Did you know that the globe was recently (in geological time), about 20,000 years ago, in the grip of an ice age? The climate goes through cycles, and we are presently in a relatively warm period before the next ice age phase. However, the global warming of the past few decades cannot be explained by this natural cycle - it is happening far too fast.

Because life can cope with slow global changes, the shifting of the continents will not likely cause stress on the animals and plants. But the effects of rapid climate change pose a serious threat of extinction to many of our fellow species, such as the Polar Bear.
