How To Make A Park

Who decides what places should become parks? And what information do they use to decide? The science of deciding where a park should be is called 'systematic conservation planning' and it's pretty complicated!

Imagine you're making the decision.

First, you'll need to gather lots of information on the animals and plants that live in nearby parks. Which species are protected already, and which need some help? You'll also need to know of any semi-pristine areas that aren't protected, and whether any of those contain threatened species.

Then you'll need to know what other purposes people might want to use the land for. Is it valuable for agriculture? Maybe governments want to use it for industry? Lots of parks are established in areas that no one else wants this isn't ideal for protecting biodiversity!

Finally, all your information is put into a computer model. This will tell you which patches of land will add most value to existing biodiversity and would cost the least to communities and governments.
