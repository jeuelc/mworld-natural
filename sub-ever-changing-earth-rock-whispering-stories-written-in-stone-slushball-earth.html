Today, the coldest places on Earth are the North and South poles. Antarctica, the frozen south polar continent, is mostly hidden under kilometre-thick ice. 'Glaciers', which are like giant frozen rivers, slowly carry ice and sediment into the surrounding ocean.

At the ends of the glaciers, the ice melts and the sediments they are carrying get dumped in a chaotic mess on the land or in the sea. The resulting rock from these messy stacks of sediments, called 'tillite', is a telltale sign that glaciers were nearby, and that the climate was terribly chilly when the rock was forming.

If you've ever been in the snow, you have a bit of an idea about how cold the world can get. However, the rocks tell us about periods in Earth's history when the world was a lot colder than today. How can the rocks tell us how cold the Earth was? You will have to check out the cards to find out...
