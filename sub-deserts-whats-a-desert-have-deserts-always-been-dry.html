Have Deserts Always Been Dry?

In many deserts you can see the remains of large rivers and lakes, as well as signs of animals and people that once lived there. Sometimes, paintings or carvings ('petroglyphs') that people made on rocks also show animals that live in or near water, like crocodiles or hippopotamuses. These signs tell us that deserts were once much wetter, and not really deserts at all.

Places can dry up and become desert as the Earth's continents move around to new locations on the Earth's surface. When ice ages happen this can also cause new deserts to appear, because during an ice age the oceans are colder and don't release much water vapour to form clouds and rain. In addition, the air is also cooler during an ice age, and can't hold as much water vapour. When the ice age ends, the desert becomes wetter again.
