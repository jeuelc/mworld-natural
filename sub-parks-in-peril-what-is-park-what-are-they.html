Where Are They?

If you added together all the protected areas of land in the world, it would make an area twice the size of Brazil (that is, about 12.7% of the worlds land area). Most countries have agreed to work together towards a target of protecting 17% of their land. In recent times we have been doing well with this target the amount of land protected has doubled in the last 20 years!

Generally the amount of land protected is higher in developing parts of the world than in developed regions. Latin America, with 20.4%, has more protected land than any other part of the world. Why do you think this might be?

The world's oceans are poorly protected by comparison, with only six million square kilometres protected. This might sound like a lot, but it is only 1.6% of our world's oceans! This is the case even though the amount protected has more than doubled over the last decade. Think about how many people rely on fish for their meals what would happen if our fish stocks were suddenly gone?

Although the number and area of protected land and sea is increasing, these areas do not yet represent the full array of the world's habitats.
