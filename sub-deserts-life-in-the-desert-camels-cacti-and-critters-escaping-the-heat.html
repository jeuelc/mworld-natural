Escaping the Heat

By living underground, people and animals can escape the heat of the desert. Soil and rock are natural insulation, and keep underground homes comfortably cool.

Desert spiders make this kind of home, resting in their burrows during the day. When it is cool, they wait just inside the entrance, and quickly dart out to capture small animals that are passing by. The animals live in a chamber at the bottom, where the temperature varies vary little from day to night, or from summer to winter. The soil is a little wetter at that depth, too, and this helps prevent the animals from drying out.

For animals that are active during the day, other adaptations help to keep them from overheating, such as pale fur to reflect sunlight, or large ears that allow their blood to loose heat into the air. Australian kangaroos lick their forelimbs when they are very hot, and the evaporation helps cool them down.
