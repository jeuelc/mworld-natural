Yellowstone

Yellowstone National Park was set up in 1872, which means that it is the world's oldest park. Yellowstone is in Wyoming in the USA and includes mountains, forest, grassland and some spectacular geysers! It is home to over 60 mammal species including bison, elk, bears and wolves.

Wolves haven't always been in Yellowstone. The species was nearly lost in the USA owing to humans hunting and trapping them and their habitat being destroyed. But between 1995 and 1997, 41 wild wolves were released into the park, mainly from Canada, and today that population has grown to more than 400 animals!

Wolves are important in the ecosystem, helping to keep the populations of their prey (elk and deer) in check. The success story of their re-introduction into the wild means that American wolves no longer face extinction and the ecosystem is more stable. If you visit Yellowstone you might just get to see one, or at least, hear it howling at night!
